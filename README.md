# Signal Infrastructure

A methodology for designing AI-assisted sensemaking systems — starting from how you actually process information, not from what tools you can connect to.

## What this is

A guide you give to Claude (or another LLM) to help you design a signal infrastructure that fits your work. It walks through a collaborative design process: mapping your existing information practice, identifying where synthesis is the real bottleneck, building a curated reading layer, and closing the feedback loop so human judgment compounds over time.

It came out of a conversation about the gap between "AI agents can scan all your systems" and the harder question underneath: what should they be looking for, and who decides what matters?

## Who it's for

Anyone whose work depends on staying ahead of signal — across industries, org sizes, and domains. The methodology is deliberately sector-agnostic. It's been shaped by conversations among people working in design, strategy, policy, security, research, media, and activism, but it doesn't assume any particular toolstack or context.

## How to use it

1. Drop [`methodology.md`](methodology.md) into a Claude Project, give it to Claude Code, or paste it into any LLM conversation
2. Start talking about how you actually stay informed — what you read, when, how you decide what matters
3. Let the methodology guide the conversation from there

The file is written as instructions to Claude, not instructions to you. Claude asks the questions. Your answers are the design spec.

## Core principle

**Attention before access.** The useful move isn't connecting an AI to all your systems. It's getting clear on what you're paying attention to and where synthesis — not access — is the bottleneck. The human decides what matters. The AI helps process the volume. The human decides what it means.
